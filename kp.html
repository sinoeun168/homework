Kampot

Kampot (Khmer: កំពត [kɑmpɔːt], lit. 'The Kampot's Fish') is a province in
southwestern Cambodia. It borders the provinces of Koh Kong and Kampong Speu to
the north, Takéo to the east, Kep and the country of Vietnam (Kiên Giang) or
Kampuchea Krom (kramuon Sor) to the south, and Sihanoukville to the west. To its
south it has a coastline of around 45 km on the Gulf of Thailand. It is rich in low arable
lands and has abundant natural resources. Its capital is the city of Kampot.

Kampot Province had a population of 627,884[2] in 2010 and consists of eight districts
divided into 92 communes with a total of 477 villages.[3] Touk Meas City is located
in the province.
